# Pastel — Sanity CMS Setup Guide

Hi Julianna! This guide walks you through setting up your content in the Sanity CMS for your new portfolio site.

## Step 1: Accept the Sanity Invite

You'll receive an email invite from Sanity to join the **Pastel** project. Click the link and follow the prompts.

- If prompted, sign in with **GitHub** (easiest option)
- You'll be taken to the Sanity dashboard

## Step 2: Access Your Content Studio

Once you're invited, go to:

```
https://pastel-navy.vercel.app/studio
```

or

```
https://sanity.io/manage → Pastel project → Launch Studio
```

You should see your content editor dashboard.

## Step 3: Add Your Portfolio Photos

### Upload Portfolio Images

1. In the studio sidebar, click **Portfolio** (or **Gallery**)
2. Click **Create new**
3. Upload a high-res photo of your makeup work
4. Add a title (e.g., "Bridal Look — Spring Wedding")
5. Add a description if you want
6. Click **Publish**

Repeat this for each portfolio piece. Photos should be:
- **High resolution** (at least 1000px wide)
- **Well-lit** (natural light is best)
- **Showcasing your work** (close-ups, full face, detail shots)

### Recommended photos to start:
- 8-12 portfolio pieces showing range
- Different looks (natural, glam, editorial, etc.)
- Bride and bridesmaid options

## Step 4: Update Your Bio

1. Click **Settings** (in the sidebar)
2. Click **About**
3. Edit your bio:
   - **Name**: Julianna
   - **Title**: "Wedding Makeup Artist"
   - **Bio**: 2-3 sentences about you, your experience, your approach
   - **Profile Photo**: Upload a professional headshot
4. Click **Publish**

### Example bio:
"I specialize in bridal and editorial makeup for couples who want to look and feel like themselves on their big day. With 10 years of experience and a focus on natural beauty, I create looks that photograph beautifully and last all night."

## Step 5: Add Your Services & Pricing

1. Click **Services** (in the sidebar)
2. Click **Create new**
3. Add a service:
   - **Name**: "Bridal Makeup" or "Wedding Day Hair + Makeup"
   - **Description**: What's included, how long the session takes
   - **Price**: e.g., "$150 per person"
4. Click **Publish**

Repeat for each service (e.g., "Engagement Session Makeup", "Trial Session", "Bridesmaid Makeup", etc.)

## Step 6: Set Up Booking Link

Your site will have a **Book Now** button that links to HoneyBook. Once HoneyBook is set up, clients can:
1. Click "Book Now" on your site
2. See your availability
3. Book directly and pay (you choose if deposit or full price)

**Note**: HoneyBook setup happens separately — ask Joe for help with that.

## Step 7: Update Your Contact Info

1. Click **Settings** → **Contact**
2. Add:
   - **Email**: Your email address
   - **Phone**: Your phone number (optional)
   - **Instagram**: Your Instagram handle (optional)
   - **Location**: Your service area (e.g., "NYC Metro")
3. Click **Publish**

This info shows up on your site's contact section.

## How It Works

Everything you publish in Sanity **automatically updates** on your live site at:

```
https://pastelmakeupandstyle.com
```

No need to ask a developer. Just edit, publish, and it's live.

## Tips

- **Save often**: Hit **Publish** after each change
- **Preview**: Click the **Preview** button to see how it looks on your site before publishing
- **Organize**: Keep portfolio items in a consistent order (maybe chronologically or by style)
- **Keywords**: Use descriptive titles and descriptions — helps with Google search when couples are looking for "wedding makeup artist near me"
